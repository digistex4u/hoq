The one-line read

Top of funnel is intact — clicks/day +2.6%, ATC-rate even held. What broke is cart → purchase (25.5% → 12.5%) and budget mix: more money pushed into the weakest collection (Cotton Trending), while the only profitable one (Qala Rajasthani / Bagru, ROAS 1.16) was starved. Net: purchases/day −52%, revenue/day −45% on higher daily spend.

02What actually changed

Five structural shifts between the two windows, ranked by impact on sales.

Cart-to-purchase halved

Add-to-cart held up (3.3% → 4.1% of landing views) but purchases per cart collapsed from 25.5% → 12.5%. People still want it; checkout/trust is where they fall off — a site/checkout issue, not a creative one.

Budget piled into the weakest page

Cotton Trending rose from 39% → 52% of spend while its ROAS fell 1.06 → 0.32 and purchases dropped 11 → 2. The biggest bet went on the worst-converting collection.

The winner got starved

Qala Rajasthani (Bagru) is the only collection above breakeven in June (ROAS 1.16), yet its budget share fell 27% → 18%. This is the page to scale, not cut.

A boost campaign is polluting the data

New "Post boosting – One exhibition" burned ₹1,058 for 87.7k impressions at 0.42% CTR, zero landing views, zero sales. It's why account CPM/CTR look "better" — pure vanity, drop it from sales reporting.

~17% of June budget in non-converting tests

Andromeda_testing (₹2,365, 0 sales) + the boost (₹1,058) = ₹3.4k with no purchases. In May, near-100% of spend sat in proven sales campaigns. Testing is fine — but not at this share during a sales dip.

Mulmul squeezed on a rising CPM

Mulmul Cotton Banarasi share fell 33% → 21%, CPM jumped ₹412 → ₹581 (+41%), purchases 7 → 1. It went quiet right as costs rose against it.

Women aged 45–65+ drive ~62–69% of all clicks and the large majority of purchases. They click at the highest rates (CTR 4.5–6.3% in May) and respond to cotton/mulmul daily-wear collections. Younger women (18–34) and all male segments together take ~30% of clicks but convert near zero — recurring spend that should be trimmed via exclusions.

Geographically it's two cities: Maharashtra (Mumbai) ~43–53% and Karnataka (Bengaluru) ~27–42% of clicks — together ~80%. South-India states (TN, Telangana, AP, Kerala) make up most of the rest.

The real signal: "Meera" didn't leave — she stopped buying. The 45–54 women drove 7 purchases in May and 0 in June; 55–64 women fell 9 → 1. Same audience, same clicks, but the cart→checkout step is failing them. That points squarely at site/checkout/trust, reinforced by the cart-conversion drop above.

05What to do this week

Audit checkout, not creatives

The drop is cart→purchase. Test the live checkout on mobile: COD availability, payment gateway errors, shipping fees revealed late, trust badges. This is the single highest-leverage fix.

Re-weight to Bagru + Mulmul

Move budget out of Cotton Trending (ROAS 0.32) into Qala Rajasthani (1.16) and rebuild Mulmul. Let the proven winner lead.

Kill the waste

Pause the "One exhibition" boost and pause/cap Andromeda_testing until the sales campaigns are stable. Recovers ~₹3.4k/5d of zero-return spend.

Fix the broken landing pages

Re-point the /search?q=mulmul ad and the FB Instant Experience ad to their proper collection pages — they currently convert nothing.
