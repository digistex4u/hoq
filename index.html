The one-line read

Blended ad spend is roughly flat (₹1.07L → ₹1.02L, −4%) but the mix shifted hard: Meta scaled +11% while Google pulled back −33%. Meta efficiency fell (ROAS 1.75 → 1.13) as budget concentrated into Cotton Trending; Google's last-click ROAS rose (15.4 → 29.1) on lower spend. These two ROAS figures describe the same Shopify orders from different angles — so the real scoreboard is store revenue, reconciled below.

05What this means & next steps

1. Rebalance Meta. Cotton Trending's 3× ramp diluted it to 1.61; cap its tail and rebuild Qala Rajasthani & Mulmul, which ran 1.8–2.3 in May.
2. Protect Google's efficiency. Google scaled down −33% yet returned more — don't starve it; it's catching high-intent demand.
3. Kill the waste. Pause the ₹1,059 boost (0 sales) and re-point the /search?q=mulmul ad (0.25 ROAS) to the Mulmul collection.
4. Finalise true blended ROAS. Reconcile Shopify net revenue (refund-netted) ÷ ₹1.02L blended spend — the one number that cuts through the Meta-vs-Google attribution overlap.
